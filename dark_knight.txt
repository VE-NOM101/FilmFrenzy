Reviewed by choyan

Batman" isn't a comic book anymore. Christopher Nolan's "The Dark Knight" is a haunted film that leaps beyond its origins and becomes an engrossing tragedy.
It creates characters we come to care about.
That's because of the performances, because of the direction, because of the writing, and because of the superlative technical quality of the entire production.
This film, and to a lesser degree "Iron Man," redefine the possibilities of the "comic-book movie.
